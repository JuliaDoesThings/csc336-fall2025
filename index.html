Why You Should Not Let Your Brother Use Your Hard Drive


A Second Website By Julia

So, you think it will be completely and totally fine to loan your spare hard drive to your brother. After all, he's an adult at this point even if you still see him as your little brother. He has been navigating technology for almost a decade now since he got his first computer - a little laptop the two of you shared - and now he's better with hardware than you are. He starts college this year, and he wants to borrow your remote hard drive because he thinks its the easiest way to move data from his old PC at home to the new one he's building at school. He's right, his terabyte of data will fit on it easily but is hard to move otherwise.
If you think he's right and you should loan him the drive, congrats, you're right where I was a month ago!



You poor, poor thing.
Do NOT loan your brother that hard drive.
You don't know what he's going to put on it. Not because he's malicious or means you harm. No, you have no idea what is about to unfold if you loan your brother that hard drive. You are about to learn that no matter how smart he is about hardware, he is stupid about software and where he downloads things from.
So no, don't sit down and run the expected mental calculus.

No wait, what are you doing?
I said don't run the mental calculus!

The Mental Calculus
Pros Cons
he's really very nice he messes around with weird software
he asked very politely he wants it for three weeks
what could go wrong? He gets a little silly with it


Okay. Are you happy now?
You still want to loan him the hard drive?

Why?

Becuase you were really convinced by the what could go wrong point?
Alright. Fine then. You fool.


What Could go wrong:

  1. It could take him way longer than three weeks to return the drive
  2. It could take him so long he has to ship it to you
  3. You have to start the semester without it
  4. The box with it arrives in the middle of the semester
  5. You plug it into your computer while working on Web Dev Homework
  6. There's ransomware on the hard drive now!

See! Look at all the things that could go very very wrong. I would know. See, it all happened to me! That's why this is my second website instead of my first. The ransomware activated in the middle of the my homework for web programming, when I pushed an final update to github. As it tore through my laptop's files, it broke the push to github. As such, the entire index file for original first website was deleted from my laptop and the index file in the github was entirely blank!

So yes, throw out the mental calculus you did. You brother will understand he can get himself his own hardrive - its probably for his best.
Protect your sanity, your essay, and your website.

Don't loan him that hard drive!
